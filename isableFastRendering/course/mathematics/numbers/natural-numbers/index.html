The Natural Numbers

Which are the Natural Numbers $\mathbb{N}$?

The Natural Numbers, denoted by $\mathbb{N}$, are the counting numbers; that is, $$1,2,3,\ldots .$$ So, $\mathbb{N}$ represents the set of all Natural Numbers; that is, $$\mathbb{N}= \{1,2,3,\ldots\}.$$ This set $\mathbb{N}$ is equal to the set of positive integers, denoted by $\mathbb{Z}^+$.

Thus, $$\mathbb{N}=\mathbb{Z}^+.$$
